LIMINAL,
A realm where the very essence of self and time finds itself in flux.
Client
Lunar Gala
Year
2024
Role
Interaction Design, 3D Web Development, Creative Direction
Lunar Gala (LG) is Pittsburgh's largest annual fashion show. It's website features the model lines, members, and ticketing all in line with this year's visual identity: Liminality.

I designed and developed the site's home page, and conceptualized the lines page. Working amongst 7 others, I learned to communicate with devs, create concept renderings, and program interactive prototypes for our Creative Directors using React Three Fiber.

We encountered challenges with members ghosting and optimization issues, but managed to release on-time, garnering 250+ attendees for the final show.

● Process

Defining Liminality.

After recieving the Creative Director's theme, Liminal, we had to establish constraints. How does LG interpret the concept of Liminality? How does that translate to digital interactions?

We define Liminality as "neither here nor there," a place that is temporary—fleeting even. It is a transitional stage... “the space between spaces” , where time cannot be measured. A sense of infinite space is created in each page.

Our challenge was to achieve Liminality through the form, function, and interaction of the website. Weekly Creative Team meetings directed our visual identity as the website had to stay cohesive with the interpretations that Print, Motion, and Photography sub-teams were developing in their work.

Various moodboards issued by CDs and creative team deliverables.

● Challenge 1: Liminality in form

What form language creates a liminal space?

The home page is a transitory white void hosting each space: Time moves in an infinite loop, and when the user wants to enter a page, they enter the “space between spaces” — neither here nor there, they are “in-between” time, and can reside in that page as long as they hold the mouse. This forced interaction was integral to evoking the feeling of temporality established in our initial definition.

A user must wait to enter a page—each space is transitory.

The people page creates an “infinity scroll” of cards representing each LG member. This emphasizes liminality and at the same time emphasizes the great mass of talents a part of making the show possible.

Seemingly endless deck of cards temporarily visible.

The lines page translates the physicality of the liminality within spaces by emulating an infinitely long fashion runway in 3D. Instead of flipping magazine-like pages, we transformed the interaction by creating depth a user must walk through using mouse position. The endless nature of the runway is innately liminal.

Infinite runway of model lines.

● Challenge 2: Liminality in function

Constructing a transitory site architecture.

The visual language and interaction design would suffer if the entire site’s mapping wasn’t transitory. This required us to establish new terms to avoid miscommunication when discussing such abstract ideas during team meetings: “Capital-L Liminal” and “liminal”—zone C—is liminality in its form—its visual language. Visual cues such as endless tunnels, negative space, and the like. “Capital-L Liminal”—zone B—is literally a liminal space—it does not exist and its only purpose is to transport the user to another space. The home page serves this purpose—it is a navigation hub as discussed earlier where time is an infinite loop. However, it is also “liminal” visually.

● Challenge 3: Liminality through Interaction

Forced interaction: Temporal mouse holds.

To enter the site, you must click and hold a panel. The nature of a mouse hold is innately temporary and transitory. Through both computer and multi-touch gestures, a mouse hold is the time-the experience even—in between the beginning and end of any interaction, encapsulating feelings of Liminality.

● Reflection

ITERATIONS ● BUGS ● R&D ● FALLEN SOLDIERS ● ...AND A PIVOT FROM DESIGNER TO CREATIVE DEVELOPER.

Notational sketching improved cross functional communciation.

Blender concept renders for home page animation.

Transitioning from designer to dev gave me eyes on both sides of the process, preparing me to lead the team next semester. Joining the team I only had some html and css background self-taught during highschool-but I still wanted to be involved in the building of the site after the design stage to ensure it's execution follows the vision. So through Blender tutorials, reading documentation, and looking towards well-structured codepen examples, I learned React and React Three Fiber to help build our site using 3D scenes.
I honed my ability to integrate my work into a large organization's brand identity. Creative team is over 40 students across different subgroups and must move as one cohesive unit. The synergistic influences during cross-team meetings and weekly general body meetings-where each team shared their progress-made me prioritize the team and themes direction in every design decision.
Notational Sketching improved my cross functional communciation. You can't talk with your hands. It allowed me to quickly communicate to another dev a simpler approach to programming the 3D infinitely rotating planes on the Home page. I took on programming and styling the texture of the Home page and frosted cursor.
